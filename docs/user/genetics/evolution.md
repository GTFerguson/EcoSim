### Death Removes Bad Genes

Natural selection works both ways:

- **Positive selection:** Helpful traits spread because their carriers reproduce more
- **Negative selection:** Harmful traits disappear because their carriers die early

**Example: Vision in a Predator-Rich Environment**

```
Low sight range â†’ Can't see predators coming â†’ Gets eaten â†’ No offspring
High sight range â†’ Spots danger early â†’ Escapes â†’ Has babies â†’ Passes on good eyes

Result: After many generations, nearly everyone has good vision!
```

## Evolution Experiments to Try

### Experiment 1: The Arms Race

1. Create a predator and prey population
2. Watch as both get faster over time
3. See who "wins" the speed competition

### Experiment 2: Island Biogeography

1. Create isolated populations (separate areas)
2. Give each area different conditions
3. Watch them diverge into different "species"

### Experiment 3: The Food Crisis

1. Establish a creature population
2. Gradually reduce food availability
3. Watch for photosynthesis activation!

### Experiment 4: Moving Plants

1. Create a plant population in one area
2. Slowly make that area worse (reduce light/water)
3. Make an adjacent area better
4. Wait for locomotion mutations!

### Experiment 5: Color Matching

1. Create a colored environment
2. Introduce randomly-colored organisms
3. Add predators
4. Watch colors converge to camouflage!

## Reading the Signs of Evolution

### âœ… Evolution IS Happening When:

- Average trait values are changing over generations
- Extreme individuals are becoming less extreme (stabilizing selection)
- OR extreme individuals are becoming more extreme (directional selection)
- New trait combinations are appearing
- Population looks different from ancestors

### âŒ Evolution is STALLED When:

- Trait values are stable for many generations
- No variation in the population (everyone's identical)
- New mutations keep dying out
- Environment is too stable (no pressure to change)

### ğŸ”„ Rapid Evolution Signs:

- Sudden environmental change
- New predator/prey introduced
- Major die-off (only best-adapted survive)
- Population bottleneck (few survivors, big genetic shifts)

## The Philosophy of EcoSim Evolution

### Emergence, Not Design

You don't design evolutionâ€”you create conditions where it can happen. The results often surprise even experienced players!

### No "Best" Organism

There's no universal "best." What's optimal depends entirely on the environment. A desert champion would fail in a swamp.

### Change is Constant

Environments change. What was perfectly adapted yesterday might be obsolete tomorrow. The most successful organisms are often generalists that can handle variety.

### Complexity from Simplicity

The most fascinating organisms in EcoSim emerge from simple rules:
- Survive â†’ Reproduce â†’ Pass on genes
- Small changes accumulate
- Selection filters constantly

From these simple principles, incredible complexity can emerge!
